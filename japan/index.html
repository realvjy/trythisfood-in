Japan

The Japanese cuisine offers many delights with a mixed variety of regional and seasonal dishes. The regional and traditional foods of Japan have developed through centuries of political, economic, and social changes. Here's what you must try...

Sushi

NON-VEG Seafood

Sushi is a Japanese dish of prepared vinegared rice, usually with some sugar and salt, accompanying a variety of ingredients, such as seafood, vegetables, and occasionally tropical fruits.

Can eat in Lunch · Dinner

Yakitori

NON-VEG

Yakitori is grilled chicken skewers made from bite-sized pieces of meat from all different parts of the chicken, such as the breasts, thighs, skin, liver and other innards. Usually made to order and cooked over charcoal, yakitori is a popular, inexpensive dish commonly enjoyed together with a glass of beer.

Can eat in Lunch · Dinner

Udon

NON-VEG Egg

Udon is a dense and chewy noodle that is made from wheat flour. It is one of the most popular foods in Japan due to its delicious taste, inexpensive price point, and versatility — udon can be eaten hot or cold and customized with any number of toppings.

Can eat in Lunch · Dinner

Sashimi

Seafood

Centuries before Japanese people were eating sushi, they first enjoyed raw fish without the rice. While the name “sashimi” refers to any thinly sliced raw food, including raw beef (gyuu-sashi), chicken (tori-zashi), and even horse (basashi), fish and seafood are the most popular varieties.

Can eat in Lunch · Dinner

Soba

VEG Non-Veg Egg

Soba is another type of noodle dish that has been eaten in Japan for centuries. Made from buckwheat flour, soba has a long thin shape and firm texture and is very healthy.

Can eat in Lunch · Dinner
